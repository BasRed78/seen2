What SEEN Is

The product, market, and opportunity

SEEN is a mental health awareness platform that helps people identify and understand their stress-response behavioral patterns before they're ready for therapy.

The core insight: When stress hits, your brain has a go-to move—texting an ex, online shopping, overworking, drinking, seeking validation. Most people don't see it as a pattern. They see individual moments of weakness. SEEN helps connect the dots.

What it's not: Therapy, diagnosis, or treatment. It's a mirror—helping people see clearly enough to take the next step.

Market Opportunity

11-year gapBetween recognizing patterns and seeking help
70%Of people who need support don't access it
90K+ waitlistsTherapy waitlists in NL alone
CompetitorsEither too generic (Headspace) or poorly executed (Liven—2.1★)

The Product

1. Free Quiz (14 questions)

Identifies one of six stress-response patterns:

  • Intimacy Seeking — Seeking connection/validation through relationships
  • Substance Soothing — Alcohol, food, cannabis
  • Compulsive Spending — Retail as emotional regulation
  • Achievement Hiding — Overwork to avoid feelings
  • Approval Chasing — External validation seeking
  • Withdrawal & Avoidance — Isolation, shutdown

2. Paid Subscription (€7.99/mo)

  • Daily AI check-ins using motivational interviewing principles
  • Proactive outreach (SEEN reaches out to you)
  • Pattern tracking and trigger identification

Current state: Product deployed at seeyourself.today

Launch target: End of January 2025

Founder Advantage & Tension ⚠️

The asset and its complications

The Founder Advantage

Bas built SEEN because he lived it. Years in a pattern he couldn't see, things falling apart, group therapy where he finally heard "me too."

The founder story is genuine, vulnerable, and uncopyable by corporate competitors like Headspace.

The Personal Tension ⚠️

There's a complication

Bas is reluctant to literally be "the face" of SEEN.

The reasons are personal and real:

  • The nature of the pattern. The founder's stress-response pattern was in a sensitive personal category — one that carries social stigma and affected his family. This isn't something he wants publicly attached to his name and face permanently.
  • Family impact. He has a son. The story involves a period that affected his family. Being the visible face means that story becomes permanently searchable, potentially affecting his son now and in the future.
  • Vulnerability limits. There's a difference between sharing the story in controlled contexts (quiz intro, therapist meetings, 1:1 conversations) and having your face on Instagram ads tied to the specific behavior.

The Strategic Question

All three expert frameworks emphasize founder-led positioning as a key differentiator. But how do you get the benefits of founder authenticity without the exposure of being literally the face?

Possible Approaches

  • Story without face: Share the narrative, but not as the visible front. Written content, not video.
  • Partial visibility: Be present in some contexts (podcasts, therapist meetings) but not in paid acquisition.
  • Anonymous founder archetype: "I built this because I wish it existed for me" without ever naming who "I" is.
  • Brand-forward, founder-backed: SEEN as the brand, with founder credibility established but not centered.
This needs to be solved. The experts all say founder story is the differentiator. But the founder has real constraints on how visible he can be. What's the right balance?

7 Strategic Questions

Where I need your input

1. Audience Strategy

One message or many?

  • Broad: "Your brain has a go-to move when life gets hard" — Cheaper, simpler, less specific
  • Pattern-specific: Six different campaigns — Higher conversion, 6x the work
  • Hybrid: Broad acquisition → pattern-specific retargeting — Best of both, more complex
Question: Start with one pattern or stay broad?

2. Transformation Statement

Current framing ("pattern awareness") is too abstract. Need concrete transformation.

Candidates:

  • "See the pattern controlling your life. Interrupt it in 30 days."
  • "Finally understand why you do what you do—and take back control."
  • "The gap between urge and action. We help you widen it."
Question: What's the one sentence that captures the value?

3. Founder-Led vs. Brand-Led

For founder-forward: Uncopyable differentiator, trust signal, story IS the marketing

For limited visibility: Personal exposure concerns, family considerations, scalability

Question: How do we get founder authenticity without full personal exposure?

4. Launch Approach

  • Hormozi: Ship now. €10/day ads. Post daily. Learn from volume.
  • Priestley: Waitlist first. 500 founding members. Create scarcity.
  • Brunson: Perfect the Epiphany Bridge. 5-day email sequence first.

Reality: Limited budget, solo founder, end-of-January target.

Question: Given constraints, what's the right launch posture?

5. Pricing Signal

Current: €7.99/month ("founding member pricing")

Options:

  • Keep €7.99, add "goes to €14.99 after 500 members"
  • Raise to €14.99 now with 30-day guarantee
  • Annual option (€79/year = 2 months free)
Question: What price positions SEEN correctly?

6. Hook Strategy

Pain-forward: "You texted your ex at 2 AM. Again. Here's why you can't stop."

Recognition-forward: "Everyone has a go-to move when life gets hard. What's yours?"

Story-forward: "I built this because I wish it existed for me."

Question: Which hook style fits the brand and audience?

7. Funnel Architecture

Current: Instagram → Quiz → Results → Pricing → Conversion

Proposed: Hook → Quiz → Teaser → Email capture → Nurture (3-5 emails) → Conversion

Question: Does this flow make sense? What's missing?

Alex Hormozi

Volume + iteration. Ship fast.

"You're overthinking and under-executing. You're being too nice to your customers by not calling out their pain hard enough. And you're being too nice to yourself by not shipping faster."

Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort)

  • Dream Outcome: You're selling "pattern awareness." People don't want awareness — they want control.
  • Perceived Likelihood: No proof. No testimonials. No case studies.
  • Time Delay: How long until results? You never say.
  • Effort: €7.99/month, few minutes/day — actually good.

Before: "SEEN helps you understand your stress-response patterns through daily AI check-ins."

After: "Stop the behavior that's destroying your life. In 30 days, you'll catch yourself before you reach for the phone, the drink, the credit card. Guaranteed."

Current: Quiz → Results → Pricing (giving away value before capturing email)

Better: Quiz → "Want the full breakdown?" → Email capture → Results + pitch → Sequence → Conversion

Add guarantee: "Try SEEN for 30 days. If you don't catch yourself at least once before acting on the pattern — full refund."

On pricing: What's the cost of the behavior? Compulsive spending = hundreds/month. And you're charging €7.99?

Low prices signal low value. Test €19.99 with a guarantee.

Rule of 100:

  • 100 pieces of content before you strategize
  • 100 quiz completions before you optimize
  • 100 subscribers before you perfect emails
  • 100 paying users before you mess with pricing

You're optimizing before you have data. Stop. Ship. Learn.

Key Takeaways

  • Reframe from process to result: "break the cycle in 30 days"
  • Quiz should capture email before full results
  • Lead with pain, not recognition
  • Volume over perfection — 100 posts before strategy
  • Price signals value — €7.99 might be too low
  • Add a guarantee to remove risk

Daniel Priestley

Authority + scarcity. Build demand.

"You're not building a mental health app. You're building a movement around a founder who lived the problem."

The 5 Ps:

  • Pitch: "I spent years stuck in a pattern I couldn't see. When it broke me, I got help. I'm building what I wish I'd had."
  • Publish: Your story IS your marketing. Tell it everywhere.
  • Product: The insight is what makes you valuable, not the features.
  • Profile: Be known as "the person who cracked stress patterns."
  • Partnership: Therapists, coaches, podcast hosts — they're amplifiers.

Principle: Demand must exceed supply before you launch.

  1. Build waitlist: "500 founding members only. Join the list."
  2. Create scarcity: "When it's full, price doubles." (Not artificial — you can't support unlimited users.)
  3. Launch to demand: 1,000 on list for 500 spots = oversubscribed.

What will still be valuable in 5 years?

  • ✅ Intellectual Property — The 6-pattern framework
  • ⚠️ Brand — Emerging, needs consistency
  • ❌ Content Library — Limited
  • ❌ Community — Doesn't exist yet
  • ❌ Partnerships — Early stage

Where's the book? The podcast? Build assets that generate leads while you sleep.

  1. Anonymous brand — No one knows who built it
  2. Founder-backed — "Built by someone who lived it"
  3. Founder-present — Known to insiders, not public face
  4. Founder-led — Face on everything

Suggestion: Level 2-3. Story leads, person stays somewhat behind it.

Priestley's Core Questions

  1. If you could only have 100 perfect users, who would they be?
  2. What would make quiz results so valuable people would pay? Give that away free.
  3. If you couldn't spend on ads, how would you reach 1,000 users?
  4. What assets will still generate leads in 5 years?

Russell Brunson

Storytelling + funnels. Belief first.

"People don't buy products. They buy access to who they want to become."

You have a legitimate Epiphany Bridge story. Every movement needs:

  1. A charismatic leader / attractive character
  2. A future-based cause
  3. A new opportunity

You have all three. But you're hiding them. The quiz leads with the pattern. It should lead with YOU.

Improvement offers are hard to sell — people have tried and failed, they're cynical.

SEEN is positioned as improvement: "Understand your patterns better."

New Opportunity: "You've tried willpower. You've tried avoiding triggers. None worked because you were solving the wrong problem. Your brain found a survival mechanism. Until you see what it's protecting you from, you'll keep reaching for the same relief. This is different."

Hook: "You text your ex when stressed. Here's why." / "Your brain has a go-to move. You probably hate it."

Story: Years in denial → Things fell apart → Group therapy, heard "me too" → Behavior wasn't the problem → Built SEEN

Offer: Not "pattern awareness." The offer: See the pattern running your life — finally understand why.

8 elements for every story:

  1. Backstory: Where were you before?
  2. Desires: What did you want?
  3. The Wall: What wasn't working?
  4. The Epiphany: The moment everything changed
  5. The Plan: What did you do differently?
  6. The Conflict: What made it hard?
  7. The Achievement: What results?
  8. The Transformation: Who did you become?

Current: Free quiz + 3 emails → €7.99/mo (not a ladder, a single step)

Build out:

  • Level 1 (Free): Quiz, story, 5-7 day sequence, sample check-in
  • Level 2 (€7.99/mo): Daily check-ins, tracking, weekly insights
  • Level 3 (€19-29/mo): Deep dive, founder access
  • Level 4 (€297+): Group cohort, live calls, 12-week program

Soap Opera Sequence: Day 1 backstory → Day 2 drama → Day 3 epiphany → Day 4 transformation → Day 5 offer

Key Takeaways

  • Rewrite landing page to lead with YOUR story
  • Position as New Opportunity, not improvement
  • Hook → Story → Offer in everything
  • Build a Value Ladder (know where €7.99 leads)
  • 5-day Soap Opera email sequence before asking for money
  • Get proof with specific timelines
